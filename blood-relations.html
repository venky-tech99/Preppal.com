Blood Relations — Logical Reasoning

What are Blood Relations problems?

Blood relations questions describe family relationships (father, mother, brother, sister, uncle, aunt, cousin, son, daughter, etc.) using short statements. The task is to deduce the relationship between two named persons or identify who is who. These require careful parsing of the given relations, tracking gender, and stepwise mapping — often drawing a quick family tree helps.

Tips: write each statement down, build the small tree, watch for words like "brother of", "daughter of", "wife of", "maternal/paternal" and consider gender clues.

1) A is the father of B. B is the mother of C. How is A related to C?

Answer: Grandfather. (A → father of B; B → mother of C → A is maternal grandfather of C)

Watch Explanation
Why: A → B (father). B → C (mother). So A is parent of B who is parent of C → A = grandparent (grandfather because A is male).

2) P is the sister of Q. Q is the mother of R. How is P related to R?

Answer: Aunt. (P is aunt of R)

Watch Explanation
Why: P and Q are siblings; Q is parent of R → sibling of parent = aunt/uncle. P is female so aunt.

3) X is the brother of Y. Y is the father of Z. How is X related to Z?

Answer: Uncle (paternal uncle).

Watch Explanation
Why: X is sibling of Z's father → paternal uncle of Z.

4) R is the son of S. T is the sister of S. How is T related to R?

Answer: Aunt.

Watch Explanation
Why: T is sibling of S (S is parent of R) → T is aunt of R.

5) F is the father of G. H is the daughter of G. How is H related to F?

Answer: Granddaughter.

Watch Explanation
Why: F → parent of G; G → parent of H → H is grandchild; H is female so granddaughter.

6) A is son of B. C is daughter of B. How is A related to C?

Answer: Brother and sister — A is brother of C.

Watch Explanation
Why: Both share same parent (B). A male child = brother of female child C.

7) M is the husband of N. N is the mother of O. How is M related to O?

Answer: Father. (by marriage/husband of mother → father of O)

Watch Explanation
Why: Spouse of a parent is also a parent (assuming conventional relations) → M = father of O.

8) V is brother of W. W is wife of X. How is V related to X?

Answer: Brother-in-law.

Watch Explanation
Why: V sibling of spouse → brother-in-law.

9) P is father of Q. R is son of S. S is daughter of Q. How is P related to R?

Answer: Grandfather. (P → parent of Q; Q → parent of S; S → parent of R → P is great-grandfather? Let's trace carefully)

Watch Explanation
Why: Q is child of P. S is child of Q (grandchild of P). R is child of S (great-grandchild of P). So P is great-grandfather of R. (Double-check tree when solving.)

10) A is father of B. C is wife of B. D is son of C. How is A related to D?

Answer: Grandfather. (A → parent of B; B & C are spouses; D is child of B & C → A is grandfather)

Watch Explanation
Why: Child of your child = grandchild. A is grandfather to D.

11) X is the sister of Y. Z is the brother of Y. How is X related to Z?

Answer: Sister — both share same parent; X is sister of Z.

Watch Explanation
Why: If two persons are siblings of the same person, they are siblings of each other (consider gender).

12) L is mother of M. M is sister of N. O is son of N. How is L related to O?

Answer: Grandmother. (L → parent of M & N; N → parent of O → L is grandmother)

Watch Explanation
Why: Parent of a parent = grandparent; L is female so grandmother.
