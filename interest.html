Quantitative Aptitude - Interest

Theory: Understanding Interest

  • Simple Interest (SI): Interest calculated only on the principal amount.
  • Formula (SI): SI = (P × R × T) / 100
  • Compound Interest (CI): Interest calculated on principal + accumulated interest.
  • Formula (CI): CI = P × (1 + R/100)T – P
  • Applications: Banking, loans, investments, and savings.
  • Tips: Convert annual rates properly for months/days.
  • Common Mistake: Mixing up SI and CI formulas.
  • Visualization: CI grows faster than SI over time.

Practice Questions with Solutions

1. Find the SI on ₹5000 at 10% per annum for 3 years.

Solution: SI = (5000 × 10 × 3) / 100 = ₹1500

3. At what rate will ₹8000 yield ₹1600 as SI in 4 years?

Solution: R = (SI × 100) / (P × T) = (1600 × 100) / (8000 × 4) = 5%

4. Find the principal if SI is ₹1200 at 8% for 3 years.

Solution: P = (SI × 100) / (R × T) = (1200 × 100) / (8 × 3) = ₹5000

5. If ₹10000 becomes ₹12100 in 2 years at CI, find the rate.

Solution: 12100 = 10000 × (1 + R/100)² → (1 + R/100)² = 1.21 → R = 10%
